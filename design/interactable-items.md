## Interactable objects 


- Some type of currency 

- Gems

- Quest items

- Keys

- Distractables


### 1. Advanced Stealth & Navigation

These objects help the player move through the environment without being detected by the "Seekers."

- **Vents and Crawlspaces:** Classic stealth staples. In Unity, these can be separate triggers that teleport the player or move them along a fixed path to a different part of the room, bypassing a guard.

- **Hiding Spots with "Peek" Mechanics:** Wardrobes, chests, or large vases. While inside, allow the player to slightly rotate the camera or "crack the door" to see the enemy's position without fully exiting.

- **Moveable Cover:** Crates or trolleys that the player can push. These can block an enemy's line of sight dynamically, allowing you to create your own "safe path" through a brightly lit room.

- **Curtains and Tapestries:** Unlike solid walls, these could be semi-transparent or sway when the player moves through them, potentially alerting a nearby enemy if they are looking closely.

### 2. Environmental Puzzles

These objects act as "gates" or logic puzzles that must be solved to progress.

- **Light Sources & Fuse Boxes:** In a hide-and-seek game, light is the enemy. Fuse boxes can be sabotaged to plunge a room into darkness, making the player harder to see but perhaps making the AI more suspicious or prone to using a flashlight.

- **Pressure Plates (Weight-Based):** Standard, but you can spice them up. Perhaps the player isn't heavy enough and needs to drag a specific object (like a heavy statue) onto it.

- **Reflective Surfaces (Mirrors):** Since you have "Gems," perhaps a specific beam of light needs to be bounced off a series of mirrors or gems to hit a sensor that opens a door.

- **Water Pumps/Valves:** Use these to change the water level in a room. This can reveal hidden items at the bottom of a pool or create floating platforms from wooden crates.


### 3. AI Manipulation & Traps

These objects allow the player to take control of the "Seeker's" behavior.

- **Sound Traps (Squeaky Floorboards/Broken Glass):** These aren't items you pick up, but interactables you must *avoid*. Alternatively, the player could intentionally step on them to lure an enemy to a specific location.

- **Remote Noise Makers:** A radio or an alarm clock. The player can set a timer or trigger it remotely to pull an enemy away from a door they are guarding.

- **Oil Slicks or Marbles:** Throw these on the floor to make an enemy slip and fall, stunning them for a few seconds. This is great for "emergency escapes" when you've been spotted.

- **Lure Food/Bait:** If your enemies are animals or monsters, you can use meat or specific plants to lead them into a cage or away from a puzzle piece.


### 4. Utility Items

Items that change how the player interacts with the world.

| **Object** | **Gameplay Function** |
| - | - |
| **Polarized Lens** | An item that, when held, reveals "invisible" footprints or paths left by enemies. |
| **Magnet** | Used to pull keys or metal quest items from across a gap or through a grate. |
| **Grappling Hook** | Allows the player to reach high-up rafters—a perfect "safe zone" in a top-down game. |
| **Smoke Bombs** | Creates a temporary AOE (Area of Effect) cloud that breaks the enemy's line of sight. |
